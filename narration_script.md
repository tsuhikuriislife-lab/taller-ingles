Today I'm going to talk to you about the project I made. It is a fishing simulator that includes a handful of lakes to choose from, different types of fish with various rarities, and a weight randomizer. It's very simple, only using loops and conditionals, along with some other basic skills like inputs and prints.

This project was born from a practice I had while talking with my partner; because of that, some things are references to our lives. It was very fun making this come to life, and I'm very proud of how it turned out.
#
Let's talk a little more in depth about the game's functionality. The game follows this simple path:

1. The game gives you a short explanation of what it can do.
2. Then, the Menu asks you if you want to play or not. If not, the game closes.
3. If you want to play, you then get to decide which lake to fish in.
4. The loop starts and you can catch cool fish like the Golden fish and the Ponyo fish (based on Ponyo from Hayao Miyazaki's anime movies!).
5. Every time you catch a fish, you can decide to go back and select another lake.

The game is very simple in terms of functionality, but it has a lot of potential and very cool ideas to be added!
#
Some things that could be added in the future may be:

1. A way to store fish in a box after you catch them.
2. A market system to sell fish and buy new equipment or buy passes to enter new lakes.
3. A more interactive fishing mechanic, like the ones in games as Stardew Valley, Animal Crossing, etc.
4. Luck mechanic that introduces day cycles, weather and pets!
